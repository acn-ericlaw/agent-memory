## What happened

- **Bootstrap chicken-and-egg.** On a fresh clone, `.kiro/` doesn't exist (gitignored, per-machine),
  and enterprise Kiro **self-bootstraps from its onboarding/MCP before reading `AGENTS.md`**. The
  maintainer's nudge **"Start from `AGENTS.md`"** put the agent-memory protocol in charge — works;
  Kiro then set up `.kiro/` and (separately) deposited enterprise hooks + steering.
- **Compatibility verdict (verified): no hard incompatibility.**
  - All of `.kiro/` is gitignored — confirmed `git check-ignore` on
    `.kiro/hooks/kiro-commit-signature.kiro.hook`, `.kiro/steering/auto-commit-prompt.md`,
    `.kiro/skills/.../SKILL.md` (all IGNORED). Enterprise content stays per-machine, never enters
    the shared `memory/`/`agent-skills/` layer.
  - `sync skill adapters` only writes `.kiro/skills/<name>/` ("never touch other files in a vendor
    dir"), so it can't clobber the enterprise hooks/steering and vice-versa.
  - **One soft tension:** an enterprise **auto-commit** hook (inferred from
    `kiro-commit-signature.kiro.hook` + `auto-commit-prompt.md`) vs. agent-memory's deliberate,
    human-gated commits. Not data corruption; a governance overlap. Per `never-pick-a-winner`, the
    human decides — accept it or scope/disable the hook.
- **Gap found + fixed.** `MIGRATE.md`'s Kiro protocol covered steering/skills/specs but **not
  hooks** — relevant because the maintainer will AI-enable other Kiro repos next.
