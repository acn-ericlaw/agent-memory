## What happened

- **Diagnosis.** The decay *rule* (`DECAY.md` §8) was correct; Copilot **miscomputed
  `sessions_since_last_used`** (counted ~5–9 as ">20") and overrode its own "still referenced in
  window" note. Root issue: counting session files by hand is the easiest review step to get wrong,
  and archival is the costliest error — yet nothing forced a check that the count matched reality.
- **Answer to the question.** General LLM misjudgment isn't eliminable without enforcement (which
  would break the no-code/lightweight design). But this *class* — over-archiving a still-referenced
  fact — is **highly catchable**, because the contradicting evidence is one `grep` away. So:
  replace the hand-counted judgment with a checkable signal for the riskiest op.
- **Shipped (maintainer chose the markdown guard over an optional lint script):** `REVIEW.md` new
  **step 6 "Verify archival"** — before stamping, `grep` the last `archive_window` session files for
  each about-to-be-archived id; **any hit ⇒ keep it** (count was wrong); confirm no id lives in both
  `continuity.md` and the archive. Added an `Archive-verify:` line to the review-summary format.
- **Scope.** `REVIEW.md` ships into targets → **MINOR**. `VERSION`→4.8.0; `UPGRADE.md` 4.7.1→4.8.0
  rung (re-sync `REVIEW.md`) + table; `README`/`CHANGELOG`. `AGENTS.md`/`SKILLS.md`/`DECAY.md` unchanged.
